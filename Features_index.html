Mindfulness Exercises

Mindful Breathing

Focus on your breath to anchor yourself in the present moment.

  • Find a comfortable position, either sitting or lying down.
  • Close your eyes and take a few deep breaths to relax.
  • Breathe in slowly through your nose, counting to four.
  • Hold your breath for a count of four.
  • Exhale slowly through your mouth for a count of six.
  • Repeat this pattern several times, focusing only on the sensation of your breath.

5-4-3-2-1 Grounding Technique

Use this technique to calm your mind and focus on your surroundings.

  • **5:** Acknowledge five things you can **see** around you.
  • **4:** Pay attention to four things you can **feel**.
  • **3:** Listen for three things you can **hear**.
  • **2:** Notice two things you can **smell**.
  • **1:** Identify one thing you can **taste**.

Simple Body Scan

This exercise helps you connect with your body and release tension.

  • Lie down or sit in a comfortable chair.
  • Close your eyes and take a few deep breaths.
  • Bring your attention to your toes, wiggling them and noticing how they feel.
  • Slowly move your focus up your body, paying attention to each part (feet, calves, thighs, etc.).
  • Notice any tension in each area and consciously try to relax it.
  • Continue until you have scanned your entire body, from head to toe.
